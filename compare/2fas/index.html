What Is 2FAS?

2FAS (Two Factor Authentication Service) is a free, open-source authenticator app known for its clean interface and privacy-first philosophy. Available on Android and iOS with a browser extension for one-tap code autofill, 2FAS has earned a loyal following. It supports TOTP, HOTP, and Steam Guard tokens, and offers biometric app locking and encrypted backups via Google Drive or iCloud.

With over 5 million downloads on Google Play, 2FAS is a solid mobile-first authenticator. However, its ecosystem limitations become evident for users who work across multiple device types. There is no standalone desktop application — 2FAS relies entirely on its browser extension paired with the mobile app for computer-based workflows. The free tier limits you to a single browser extension connection, with the $9.99/year 2FAS Pass upgrade required for unlimited extensions, sync, and autofill.

Cross-platform sync is locked to your mobile ecosystem: Android users sync via Google Drive, iOS users via iCloud. There is no universal cross-platform sync between Android and iOS without the paid tier.

Platform Availability: Mobile-First vs Truly Cross-Platform

2FAS takes a mobile-first approach: the core experience lives on your phone, and the browser extension serves as a bridge to get codes on your computer. This works for basic use cases, but it means you always need your phone nearby. If your phone is charging in another room, you're locked out.

TwoFac takes a different philosophy — every platform is a first-class citizen. Native desktop apps for macOS, Windows, and Linux run independently with their own local storage and sync. The CLI tool generates codes from any terminal. The PWA works in any modern browser. And companion apps for Wear OS and Apple Watch let you view codes on your wrist.

Security and Privacy: Two Open-Source Champions

Both TwoFac and 2FAS take security seriously. Both are fully open source, both offer biometric app locking, and both generate codes locally without server interaction. Neither app collects personal data or requires an account.

Where they diverge is in backup encryption and sync strategy. 2FAS encrypts backups and stores them on Google Drive (Android) or iCloud (iOS) — tying your backup security to your Google or Apple account. TwoFac uses end-to-end encryption for sync and backup with keys derived from your passphrase that never leave your devices.

Pricing: Free Core vs Completely Free

2FAS's core authenticator app is free with unlimited token storage. However, the free tier limits you to a single browser extension connection. The 2FAS Pass upgrade ($9.99/year) unlocks cross-platform sync, unlimited browser extensions, and autofill capabilities.

TwoFac provides end-to-end encrypted sync across all devices regardless of platform — Android, iOS, desktop, web, and wearables all stay in sync automatically. There is no premium tier. Every sync and backup feature is free on day one.
