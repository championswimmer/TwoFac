What Is Proton Authenticator?

Proton Authenticator is Proton's standalone 2FA app built around the same privacy principles as Proton Mail and Proton Drive. It is open source, works offline, supports app locking, and offers end-to-end encrypted sync across Android, iPhone, Windows, macOS, and Linux.

It is a much more modern option than legacy authenticators because it avoids ads, supports import/export, and lets you use it locally without creating an account. For users who mainly want a clean mobile + desktop authenticator with secure sync, Proton Authenticator is a compelling choice.

TwoFac differs by expanding the surface area: web, browser extensions, CLI tooling, and smartwatch apps are all first-class citizens. If your 2FA life extends beyond phone and desktop windows, TwoFac gives you more ways to get to the same vault.

Shared Strengths: Open Source, Private, Cross-Platform

This is not a story of an outdated incumbent versus a modern challenger. Proton Authenticator is modern too: open source, encrypted, cross-platform, and designed to avoid vendor lock-in.

That makes the comparison more interesting. If your shortlist already includes Proton, you are clearly looking for a privacy-first authenticator. The decision then comes down to reach and workflow, not whether one app takes security seriously and the other doesn't.

Where TwoFac Pulls Ahead

Proton Authenticator has no browser extension, no PWA, no CLI, and no wearable apps. If you mostly live inside desktop windows, that's fine. But if you bounce between browsers, terminals, watches, and multiple types of devices, the missing surfaces become obvious very quickly.

TwoFac covers 11+ platforms and includes dedicated clients for browser autofill, web access, command-line workflows, Wear OS, and Apple Watch. It turns your authenticator into an everywhere tool rather than a desktop/mobile pair.

Who Should Pick Which?

Proton Authenticator is a great fit if you want a straightforward encrypted authenticator for mobile and desktop and you already trust Proton's privacy model.

TwoFac is the better fit if you want the same privacy posture but need more reach: browser autofill, terminal access, smartwatch companions, web installability, and a single toolchain that adapts to how you actually log in throughout the day.
