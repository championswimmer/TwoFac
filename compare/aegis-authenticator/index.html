What Is Aegis Authenticator?

Aegis Authenticator is a free, open-source 2FA app for Android focused on doing one job extremely well: keeping your TOTP and HOTP tokens in a locally encrypted vault. Its vault uses strong encryption, supports biometric unlock, blocks screenshots, and offers one of the best import/export toolsets in the Android authenticator ecosystem.

Aegis also includes features many authenticators still miss: automatic backups to a folder of your choice, custom icons, grouping, search, QR/image import, and broad compatibility with exports from other apps. For Android-only users who prefer a strictly local workflow, it's an excellent choice.

Where Aegis falls short is platform reach. There is no iPhone app, no desktop companion, no web client, no browser extension, and no smartwatch support. TwoFac picks up exactly where Aegis stops.

Platform Coverage: Android Specialist vs Full Ecosystem

Aegis is unapologetically Android-first. That focus helps it feel polished on Android, but it also means your vault stays trapped on one platform unless you manually shuttle backup files around.

TwoFac gives you the same authenticator experience on 11+ platforms: Android, iPhone, macOS, Windows, Linux, web, browser extensions, CLI, Wear OS, and Apple Watch. If you log in from more than one device type, this difference is enormous.

Security Philosophy: Local Vault vs Optional Encrypted Sync

Aegis wins a lot of trust because it is simple and local-first: your secrets live in an encrypted Android vault, and backups happen only where you point them. No account, no analytics, no mandatory cloud.

TwoFac shares the same privacy posture by default, but adds end-to-end encrypted sync for people who need multiple devices. You keep the security properties of encrypted storage while avoiding the pain of manual export and restore every time you add a new device.

Why Power Users Outgrow Aegis

Aegis gives Android users excellent vault management, but it cannot help when you need a code on your Linux desktop, inside a browser login form, on a remote SSH session, or from your smartwatch while your phone is charging.

That's where TwoFac stands apart: browser autofill, a real CLI, native desktop apps, wearable clients, and a PWA all work together as one authenticator instead of forcing you back to your phone for every login.
